COMPOUND KEY: 
	Multiple columns can uniquely identify the record, and also foreign key.
	Two or more columns, used for indexing purposes as well.
	Can use foreign key as a column.

COMPOSITE KEYS: 
	Multiple columns can uniquely identify the record, and also foreign key.
	Cannot use foreign key as a column.


SURROGATE KEYS: 
	Surrogate keys are just like primary keys.
	They are artificially generated by the DBMS.
